🛡 MailSentry

Privacy policy

Effective 2026-06-26

MailSentry is a Chrome extension that flags likely scam emails inside Gmail. It is designed so the contents of your inbox never leave your machine.

What MailSentry reads from your Gmail

When you have an email open in Gmail, MailSentry reads the following from the page locally, inside your browser:

  • Sender email address and display name
  • Subject line
  • Body text (scanned for urgency/pressure keywords)
  • Links inside the body (<a href> URLs)
  • Attachment filenames (presence and type only)
  • Images in the body (for QR-code decoding)

What leaves your device

DataWhere it goes & why
URLs extracted from the email body and QR codes Sent to the Google Safe Browsing API to check against known phishing and malware lists. The URL itself is the only data sent — no sender, subject, body, or attachment information is included. Requires you to provide your own Safe Browsing API key in the popup; without a key, no URLs are sent.
Everything else Nothing else leaves your machine.

What stays on your device

All of the following processing happens entirely inside your browser, with no external API call:

  • Levenshtein (lookalike-domain) comparison against your saved contacts
  • Urgency keyword scoring of the subject and body
  • Attachment-on-payment-email check
  • QR-code decoding (when enabled in a future build)
  • Composite risk score and the plain-language banner shown above the email

What MailSentry never does

  • No MailSentry server. There is no backend that receives your email data.
  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking.
  • No account, no sign-up, no identifiers.
  • No selling, sharing, or storage of email content anywhere.
  • No reading of emails you have not opened.

What is stored, and where

MailSentry uses chrome.storage.local on your own device to store:

  • Your trusted-contacts list (the vendors/emails you add in the popup)
  • Your allowlist entries and on/off toggle
  • Your Google Safe Browsing API key (if you entered one)
  • Whether you accepted the first-run consent screen

This data stays on your machine. Uninstalling the extension removes it.

We designed MailSentry client-side specifically so you never have to trust us with your inbox. The only data that ever leaves your device is the URLs we ask Google Safe Browsing to check — and you control whether that happens by choosing whether to add a Safe Browsing key.

Third-party service used

Google Safe Browsing — Google's URL reputation service. When MailSentry sends a URL to this API, it is subject to Google's data handling for that service. See safebrowsing.google.com for details.
